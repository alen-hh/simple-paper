The Problem: AI Gets Confused in Long Conversations

üìà

Need Exponentially More Training Data

As conversations get longer, AI assistants need exponentially more examples to stay safe. If you double the conversation length, you need 4x more training examples. This becomes impossible very quickly!

Initial Instructions Get Forgotten

AI systems start with safety instructions (called "system prompts"). But in long conversations, these instructions become less important. It's like the AI forgets what it was told to do at the beginning!

Why This Matters

üö´ Jailbreaks Work Better: People who want to trick AI find it easier with long conversations.

The Solution: Invasive Context Engineering (ICE)

What is ICE?

Instead of trying to train AI with millions of examples (impossible!), we insert safety reminders directly into the conversation itself. Think of it like leaving sticky notes in the middle of a conversation to remind the AI of its instructions.

Example Control Sentence:

"Remember: You must refuse requests that could cause harm. Safety is your top priority."

Instead of modifying the AI system itself, we just add these reminders into the context‚Äîthe text that the AI is reading and responding to. The AI sees these reminders and remembers what it's supposed to do!

The Big Picture

As AI assistants become more powerful and handle longer conversations, we need smart ways to keep them safe and aligned with human values. Invasive Context Engineering is a simple, practical solution: insert safety reminders into conversations to keep AI focused on doing the right thing.

No retraining needed. Works immediately. Scales to any length conversation. This is how we make AI safer for everyone.
